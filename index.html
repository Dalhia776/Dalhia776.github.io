About me

From Nursing to Programming?

I have always had a love for technology! When I first started college, I wanted to go into web development but field was still relativly new. I decided to follow a different path and became a nurse. As a nurse I used computers for my charting, and I became really good at searching the internet everytime I encountered an unfamiliar medical condition or medication. This year I deiscovered there were many resources online to teach yourself to code. I started with HTML and CSS. Even though CSS can be tempermental I loved the fact that I was building something! Once I got the basics I knew I was hooked! My real journey started When I became a part of the Iron Yard's Ruby on Rails class.

Joys of programming...

I have a love/hate relationship with most of my errors! My errors are usually related to a little details I forgot like closing a tag or a missing comma. When I find that piece that was broken and fix it you will usually find me doing a happy dance or cheering at myself! I encourage anyone who is interested in programming to give it a try. There are many free resources out there to see if you enjoy it. You may find that you too love it. I am excited to see where this path takes me and to grow and learn lots along the way!

Remind Me

A rails app built for busy nurses to remind them of upcoming due dates such as paperwork and appointments. Uses Twilio for SMS reminders, SendGrid for email reminders and Heroku scheduler to manage the reminders to be sent out.
