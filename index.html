About me

How did I choose programming?

I have always had a love for technology! When I first started college, I wanted to go into web development. The field was still relativly new and as a parent I decided to follow a different path and became a nurse. As a nurse I began to use computers for my charting as well as learning to use the internet to search for what certain medical conditions and medications were. This past year was the year I decided to learn more about how web pages and programs were built. I always wondered if development would be too hard for me to understand and I realize it is a challlenge but not hard to understand if you keep practicing! After I realized I understood the basics I was hooked!

Where I hope my career will go!

I have always loved problem solving. As a nurse I woud listen to patients concerns and with the help of the physician try to figure out what the problem was. Programming works much the same way. Attention to detail is key when dealing with development! Most errors are related to little details like forgetting to close a tag or even missing a comma. I find myself encouraging others who are interested in this career path and anyone who wants to learn I try share whatever ways I know to help them learn. I am excited and eager to grow and learn not just in Ruby on Rails but with the many other languages and technologies. The list is limitless!

Remind Me

A rails app built for busy nurses to remind them of upcoming due dates such as paperwork and appointments. Uses Twilio for SMS reminders, SendGrid for email reminders and Heroku scheduler to manage the reminders to be sent out.
